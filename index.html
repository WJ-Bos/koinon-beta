What is Koinon

Spiritual infrastructure
for the global church

Koinon takes its name from the Greek koinōnia — the mutual participation of the early church described throughout the New Testament. Not a social platform. Not a content app. Spiritual infrastructure.

01

One Public Bible

Every verse in Scripture is a shared space. The community leaves annotations — not on a feed, but in the margins of the Word itself. Notes anchor to universal verse references, so a note left in the WEB appears for the BSB reader on the same passage.

02

Daily Rhythm

The entire platform reads the same passage on the same day. No personal progress tracker. No liturgical calendar. No curated selection. A book is chosen, the community reads it together, and when it ends, another begins. Providence, not preference.

03

Church Communities

Churches create private or public communities for their congregation. Leaders post weekly themes, share Sunday's passage in advance, and list church events — all in the same space where members do their daily reading.

What Koinon
is not

These are permanent decisions — not roadmap deferrals. The platform's spiritual posture depends on them.

  • A social media platform — no feeds, likes, follows, or follower counts
  • A content platform — Koinon never publishes its own devotionals or branded content
  • Freemium — every user receives the same experience, always
  • Denominational — no theological position, no tradition over another
  • Gamified — no streaks, no leaderboards, no progress that resets when life interrupts
  • A church replacement — a complement to real community, not a substitute for it

Core Features

Built around
what matters

📖

The Annotated Scripture

Tap any verse to see what the community found there — testimonies in the margins, confessions beneath passages about grace, prayers attached to promises. Over time the Bible becomes annotated by lived experience.

🕊

Anonymous Prayer Wall

Submit a prayer without attaching your identity. Others mark that they prayed. You receive a quiet notification — someone stood with you — but who is never stored or revealed. Anonymous by architecture, not policy.

🔥

Presence Counter

A small number on today's passage shows how many people have paused here today. No names. No profiles. Just the quiet knowledge that you are not reading alone.

⛪

Church Communities

Private communities stay hidden from the world — joinable only by invite link or code. Public communities appear in discovery, letting a well-known pastor's congregation find each other naturally on the platform.
